The Last Supper

(1490s)

L'Ultima Cena

The Last Supper is a late 15th-century mural painting by Italian artist
Leonardo da Vinci housed by the refectory of the Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie
in Milan, Italy. It is one of the Western world's most recognizable paintings.



The work is assumed to have been started around 1495–96 and was commissioned
as part of a plan of renovations to the church and its convent buildings by
Leonardo's patron Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan.
