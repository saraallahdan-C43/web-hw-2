The Grate Wave

(1829–1833)

Katsushika Hokusai

The Great Wave or simply The Wave, is a woodblock print by
the Japanese ukiyo-e artist Hokusai. It was published sometime between 1829
and 1833 in the late Edo period as the first print in Hokusai's series Thirty-six
Views of Mount Fuji.


The image depicts an enormous wave threatening three boats off the coast in the
Sagami Bay (Kanagawa Prefecture) while Mount Fuji rises in the background.
Sometimes assumed to be a tsunami, the wave is more likely to be a large rogue wave.
