The Scream

(1893)

Edvard Munch

The Scream is the popular name given to a composition created
by Norwegian Expressionist artist Edvard Munch in 1893. The original German
title given by Munch to his work was Der Schrei der Natur (The Scream of Nature),
and the Norwegian title is Skrik (Shriek).


Munch recalled that he had been out for a walk at sunset when
suddenly the setting sunlight turned the clouds "a blood red". He sensed an
"infinite scream passing through nature".
